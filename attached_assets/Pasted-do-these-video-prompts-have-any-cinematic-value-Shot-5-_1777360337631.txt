Short answer: No — not really. They sound cinematic, but they don’t actually behave like cinema.
Let me explain properly, because this is exactly where most AI video systems fail (and where your Qaivid vision is actually right).

🔴 What’s wrong with these prompts
1. ❌ They are not shots — they are filters
You’ve written:
* “Apply warm colour grade”
* “slight vignette”
* “nostalgic mood”
These are post-processing instructions, not cinematic shot direction.
👉 A real shot answers:
* What is happening?
* Who is in frame?
* What is the camera doing?
* What is the emotional beat?
Right now, your “Action” is not an action at all.

2. ❌ All shots are identical → zero progression
Shot 5, 6, 7 = same:
* Same mood
* Same lighting
* Same micro-detail
* Same instructions
👉 Cinema is change over time Right now this is just:
“Hold the same vibe for 3 shots”
That kills narrative energy.

3. ❌ No subject, no intention, no framing
There is:
* No character
* No physical action
* No framing (close-up? wide? profile?)
* No camera logic (push, pull, static, handheld?)
👉 Without this, the model will hallucinate randomly.

4. ❌ “Avoid” section is overloaded and confused
You wrote:
* avoid fast cuts
* avoid dark themes
* avoid urban settings
* natural cinematic motion (this is not even an “avoid”)
👉 This creates conflicting constraints Models either ignore it or produce safe, boring output.

5. ❌ No emotional beat change
All shots say:
“nostalgic atmosphere builds”
But:
* builds how?
* what changes between 5 → 6 → 7?
👉 Cinema = emotional progression per shot

🟢 What cinematic prompts actually look like
Here’s how your same idea should be written properly:

✅ Shot 5 (Establish internal state)
Action: Woman stands still, looking toward distant horizon, eyes slightly unfocused as if lost in memory Framing: medium 3/4 body, subject slightly off-center Camera: slow subtle push-in (almost imperceptible) Lighting: soft early morning light from side, gentle highlights on face Mood: quiet emotional heaviness, controlled stillness Micro-detail: fabric moves slightly in breeze, slow breath visible

✅ Shot 6 (Internal shift)
Action: Her gaze drops slightly, eyelids soften as emotion deepens Framing: tighter — chest to head Camera: static hold (no movement now) Lighting: same light but slightly warmer tones emerging Mood: nostalgia turning into personal ache Micro-detail: slight lip tension, micro eye movement

✅ Shot 7 (Emotional peak hint)
Action: She blinks slowly, almost holding back emotion Framing: close-up on face Camera: very slow push-in resumes Lighting: warm glow increases subtly on skin tones Mood: emotional threshold — not breaking, but close Micro-detail: moisture in eyes, breath slightly heavier

🔥 The real issue (important for Qaivid)
What you wrote is style-layer prompting What you need is intent-layer prompting
You’re currently doing:
“Make it look cinematic”
But cinema is:
“What is happening, and how does the camera reveal it over time”

🧠 Key Rule (this is gold for your system)
One shot = one emotional micro-change + one visual priority
Right now:
* ❌ You have zero micro-change
* ❌ Multiple vague visual ideas
* ❌ No subject focus

Your prompts have:
* ✅ aesthetic awareness
* ✅ tonal consistency
But they lack:
* ❌ cinematic grammar
